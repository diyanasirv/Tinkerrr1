After Birth Recovery Guide

Postpartum Recovery Progress

The postpartum period lasts around **6 weeks to 6 months**. Your body gradually heals, and you adjust to life with a newborn.

50% Recovered

Recovery Timeline: Week-by-Week

Week 1-2: Initial Healing & Rest

Your body is still healing. Expect **heavy bleeding, soreness, and fatigue**.

Week 3-6: Strength & Hormonal Adjustments

Bleeding reduces, and you may feel **physically stronger**, but mood swings are common.

Beyond 6 Weeks: Full Recovery

Most women feel **significantly better**, but full recovery (especially after a C-section) may take months.

Newborn Care Essentials

Taking care of your baby involves feeding, sleep routines, and health monitoring.

  • Feeding: Breastfeed every **2-3 hours**, or formula-feed as needed.
  • Sleep: Babies sleep **16-18 hours daily** but wake frequently.
  • Umbilical Cord Care: Keep the stump dry; it falls off in **1-2 weeks**.

Postpartum Nutrition

Eating the right foods can help with recovery and breastfeeding.

  • Oatmeal & Whole Grains: Boosts energy and milk supply.
  • Leafy Greens: Provides **iron** to recover from blood loss.
  • Salmon & Eggs: Supports **brain function** and mental health.

Gentle Postpartum Exercises

Start light exercises after consulting your doctor.

  • Walking: Improves circulation and relieves stress.
  • Pelvic Floor Exercises: Helps strengthen muscles after childbirth.
  • Postnatal Yoga: Aids flexibility and emotional well-being.

Emotional & Mental Well-being

Hormonal changes can lead to **mood swings, anxiety, or postpartum depression**.

  • Get enough **rest** and ask for support.
  • Join a **new mom support group**.
  • Talk to a **therapist if you feel overwhelmed**.

Medical Check-up Schedule

Regular check-ups ensure a smooth recovery.

  • Week 1-2: C-section or stitches check.
  • Week 6: Full postpartum check-up.
  • For Baby: Vaccination and growth monitoring.

FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

When can I start exercising again?

Most women can start light activity after **6 weeks**.

How do I know if I have postpartum depression?

Persistent sadness, extreme fatigue, and detachment from the baby are key signs.
